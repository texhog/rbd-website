Learn to Play

Everything you need to know to run a Resilience by Design workshop

About the Game Learn to Play Score a Game

Tips for Success

  • Communicate openly: Share your role's particular strengths with the team. Each role has unique abilities that can benefit everyone.
  • Remember you can trade: Trading tokens between players (once per turn, by mutual agreement) can unlock projects faster.
  • Just start somewhere: The game only lasts an hour, so don't overthink your first moves. Jump in!
  • Build toward bigger projects: As you complete Level 1 projects and collect permanent bonuses, you'll unlock the ability to afford Level 2 and Level 3 projects‚Äîand eventually achieve your Community Resilience Goals.

Resilience by Design is a serious game focused on climate change planning, adaptation, mitigation, and resilience. It blends gameplay with learning to build shared understanding while practicing collaboration, communication, and planning under pressure.

The Workshop Structure

  1. Pre-game Workshop: Choose hazards, co-create community resilience goals, and establish a shared points target.
  2. 1-Hour Play Session: A timed cooperative session simulating one fiscal year.
  3. Facilitated Discussion: Review which goals were achieved and what could change.
  4. Planning Activity: Work with achieved goals using the Community Resilience Planning Canvas.
  5. Debrief: Discuss how to apply lessons learned to real-world contexts.

Game Objective

Work together to reach or exceed the community target (points) within 1 hour. The base target is 56 points. Each hazard selected adds to this target.

Winning & Losing

This is a collaborative game‚Äîyou win or lose together as a team! If the team total meets or beats the shared target when time expires, the community succeeds. If not, discuss what could be improved and consider the optional 15-minute extension.

Why Reserve Cards?

Reserving a project card is a strategic action with two key benefits:

  1. Secure a project for yourself: When you reserve a card (face-up or blind from the deck), it goes to your Reserved Projects area. No other player can complete it‚Äîit's yours alone to finish on a future turn.
  2. Gain Wild Aid: Reserving is the only way to gain Wild Aid tokens! Each time you reserve, you take 1 Wild Aid from the bank (if available). Wild Aid can substitute for any resource type when completing projects.

Reserve strategically when you see a valuable project you can't afford yet, or when you need Wild Aid to complete something else. Remember: most roles can hold up to 3 reserved projects (Local Government and Local Authority can hold 4).

Resource Tokens

Colour-coded tokens representing 6 categories of resources:

Volunteers (Cyan) Community members contributing time and effort
Knowledge (Green) Expertise, research, and information sharing
Funding (Red) Financial resources and grants
Materials (Gray) Physical supplies and equipment
Political Will (Black) Government support and policy backing
Wild Aid (Yellow) Flexible resources that can substitute for any other type

Begin with 7 of each standard color and 5 Wild Aid in the resource bank.

Project Cards (80 total)

  • Level 1 - Local Capacity (40 cards): Low cost, 0-1 points, grants 1 bonus icon. Emphasis on capacity building.
  • Level 2 - Scaling Capacity (30 cards): Medium cost, 1-2 points, grants 1-2 bonus icons. Balanced mix.
  • Level 3 - Resilient Systems (10 cards): High cost, 3-5 points, grants 2 bonus icons. Emphasis on systems.

Understanding Project Cards

Each project card has three key elements:

  • End Game Points (top left): Points you earn when the game ends. Add all points from completed projects to your score.
  • Permanent Bonuses (right side): Resource icons you can use to complete goals or other projects. These bonuses stay active once the project is completed.
  • Cost (bottom): Resources needed to complete the project. Pay these tokens to the bank when completing the project.
Level 1 Example

Level 1 cards typically have 0-1 end game points, grant 1 permanent bonus, and have low costs (1-3 resources).

Level 2 Example

Level 2 cards typically have 1-2 end game points, grant 1-2 permanent bonuses, and have medium costs (3-5 resources).

Level 3 Example

Level 3 cards typically have 3-5 end game points, grant 2 permanent bonuses, and have high costs (5-8 resources).

Scoring Reminder

In the Player Scores table: Enter the total point value from all completed project cards of each level, not the number of cards. For example, if you completed three Level 1 projects worth 1 point each, enter "3" in the Level 1 Projects column.

  • Hazard Cards: 12 total. Six hazards, each with Minor and Major versions.

2. Co-Create Resilience Goals

Using the Cross-System Impacts & Resilience Goals worksheet, teams identify:

  • Local impacts for each hazard
  • Local vulnerabilities and relevant capacities across 6 value areas (A-F)
  • Custom resilience goals with short titles

  • B: Built Environment
  • C: Natural Environment
  • D: Economy
  • E: Governance
  • F: Health & Wellbeing

3. Calculate Target Score

Final Target = Base (56) + Minor Hazards (+2 each) + Major Hazards (+5 each)

Example: Base 56 + Major Wildfire (+5) + Major Flood (+5) + Minor Drought (+2) = 68 target

4. Table Setup

  1. Assign Roles: Choose 4 of the 7 available stations/roles.
  2. Prepare Resource Tokens: Take 7 of each standard color and 5 Wild Aid from the box.
  3. Review Station Rules: Each player reviews their player mat's unique abilities.
  4. Starting Resources: Each player takes the starting tokens listed on their mat.
  5. Common Supply: Place remaining tokens in a common area within reach of all players.
  6. Reveal Projects: Shuffle each level separately. Reveal 4 face-up cards per level (3 rows).
  7. Place Goals: Display the 6 goal cards where all can see them.

Start a 1-hour timer. Choose a first player. Play proceeds clockwise.

On Your Turn (in order):

  1. Perform exactly one main action (Take Resources, Reserve a Project, or Complete a Project)
  2. Optional: Use your station's once-per-turn special power
  3. Optional: Make one trade offer using your station's trade power (consent required)
  4. Optional: Claim at most one goal using permanent bonuses only
  5. End of Turn: Check token limit (max 10), refill project slots, pass to next player

Important Limits

  • Maximum 10 tokens per player at end of turn (including Wild Aid). Discard extras back to supply.
  • Projects refill immediately when taken or completed.
  • Keep communicating with your team throughout!

On your turn, choose exactly one of these main actions:

1. Take Resources

  • Take 3 different tokens from the supply
  • Province role may take 4 different tokens instead of 3

2. Reserve a Project

Reserving is the only way to get Wild Aid tokens. Here's how it works:

  • Take 1 face-up project from any level OR draw 1 blind from any deck
  • Place it in your "Reserved Projects" area on your player mat
  • Gain 1 Wild Aid token (if available in the supply)

Why Reserve a Project?

  • Get Wild Aid: This is the ONLY way to obtain Wild Aid tokens. Wild Aid can substitute for any resource type when completing projects.
  • Reveal new cards: When you take a face-up card, it gets replaced from the deck. This shows new project options for yourself or teammates.
  • Strategic hold: You're close to completing a project but short on resources. Reserve it now so no one else takes it, then complete it on a future turn using Wild Aid to cover what you're missing.

Reserve Limits:

  • Standard: 3 reserved projects maximum
  • Local Government / Local Authority: 4 reserved projects maximum
  • Wild Aid cap varies by role (check your player mat)

Reserved projects are only yours - no other player can complete or buy them.

3. Complete a Project

  • Pay the cost listed on the project using tokens AND/OR permanent bonuses
  • Wild Aid tokens can substitute for any resource type
  • Place the completed card face-up in the matching "Completed Projects" area on your mat (sorted by bonus color)
  • Gain the project's printed points
  • The card's color = the permanent bonus type you gained

Understanding Permanent Bonuses

When you complete a project, it gives you a permanent bonus icon matching the card's color border. These bonuses have TWO uses:

  • For Projects: Reduce the token cost of future projects. Each bonus icon of a color reduces that resource cost by 1.
  • For Goals: Goals can ONLY be claimed using permanent bonuses - tokens in hand don't count!

Count your completed projects in each color area on your mat to see your permanent bonuses.

Choose 4 of 7 available stations to sit at the table. Each station has unique abilities. One or more people may share a station and act as a team.

Understanding Your Player Mat

Each player mat has several important sections:

1 Points Track (Left Side)

Track your score from 1-25 as you complete projects. Also shows the 10-token maximum reminder.

2 Role Identity & Abilities (Center Top)

Shows your role name, starting resources, and unique player abilities that apply only to you.

3 Reserved Projects (Top Right)

Place face-up or blind projects here when you reserve them. These are yours alone‚Äîno one else can complete them. Limit: 3 cards (or 4 for Local Government/Authority).

4 Common Game Rules (Center Right)

Quick reference for the three main actions: Take Resources, Reserve a Project, and Complete a Project.

5 Completed Projects (Bottom)

Place finished project cards here, organized by resource type. The icons at the bottom track your permanent bonuses‚Äîthese count toward Community Resilience Goals!

üí° Important: Permanent bonuses (icons on completed projects) are different from tokens in your hand. Only permanent bonuses count toward claiming Community Resilience Goals!

The 7 Player Roles

Local Government

Starting Resources: 1 Materials, 1 Volunteers

  • May reserve up to 4 projects (instead of 3)
  • When you complete a Materials project: gain +1 Volunteers token
  • Once per turn: convert 1 Materials ‚Üí 1 Volunteers
  • Wild Aid cap: 2
Trade Power - Contract Services: Offer 1 Materials to another player. If accepted, receive 1 Volunteers.

Local Authority

Starting Resources: 1 Materials, 1 Volunteers

  • May reserve up to 4 projects (instead of 3)
  • When you complete a Materials project: gain +1 Volunteers token
  • Once per turn: convert 1 Materials ‚Üí 1 Volunteers
  • Wild Aid cap: 2
Trade Power: Offer 1 Materials to another player. If accepted, receive 1 Volunteers.

Business and Industry

Starting Resources: 2 Funding

  • Pay 1 fewer Funding on all Level 2 and Level 3 projects
  • When you complete a Funding project: draw 1 random token (d6 rule)
  • Once per turn: pay 1 Funding to draw 1 random token (d6 rule)
  • Wild Aid cap: 1
Trade Power: Offer 2 Funding to a player. If accepted, draw 1 random token using the d6 rule.

Infrastructure Owners

Starting Resources: 1 Materials, 1 Funding

  • Pay 1 fewer Materials on Level 2 and Level 3 projects
  • When you complete a Materials project: draw 1 random token (d6 rule)
  • Wild Aid cap: 1
Trade Power: Offer 1 Materials to a player. If accepted, they return 1 Funding or 1 Knowledge.

First Nations

Starting Resources: 1 Knowledge

  • Once per turn: treat 1 Knowledge as Political Will while paying a cost
  • When a project cost includes both Knowledge and Political Will: gain +1 point
  • Wild Aid cap: No limit
Trade Power: Give 1 Knowledge to a player. If accepted, they return 1 Political Will or 1 Volunteers.

Province

Starting Resources: 1 Political Will

  • When taking resources: may take 4 different tokens instead of 3
  • Once per round, Program Transfer: Offer 1 token to a player. If accepted, they must immediately reserve or complete a project.
  • Wild Aid cap: 2
Trade Power: Offer 1 Funding to a player. If they complete a project this turn, take 1 non-Wild token from the supply.

Community

Starting Resources: 1 Volunteers, 1 Knowledge

  • Once per turn: treat 1 Volunteers as 1 Materials while paying a cost
  • When you complete a Volunteers project: gain 1 Knowledge token (if available)
  • Wild Aid cap: 2
Trade Power: Offer 1 Volunteers to a player. If accepted, they return 1 Materials or 1 Funding.

Key Rules for Goals

  • Each goal is worth 6 points
  • Goals are claimed using permanent bonuses ONLY (from completed projects)
  • Tokens in hand do NOT count toward goal requirements
  • You may claim at most 1 goal per turn

How to Achieve Goals

Goals are achieved by completing project cards on your player mat. Here's how it works:

Important Restrictions
  • Each player must have the required projects completed on their own player mat
  • Projects may NOT be traded, shared, or pooled to achieve a goal
  • Only the projects on an individual player's mat may be used to meet a goal's requirements
  • Tokens may NOT be used to achieve goals; only completed projects and their permanent bonuses count

To achieve a goal: Count the permanent bonuses (resource icons) on your completed project cards. If you have enough of the required types, you may claim that goal on your turn and immediately add 6 points to your score.

When the Timer Expires

The game ends when the 1-hour timer expires. This represents the end of the fiscal year.

Calculate Final Score

  1. Total all project points from completed projects (all players)
  2. Add all goal points (6 points each)
  3. Compare total to your target score

Win or Lose

  • Meet or exceed target: The community wins together! üéâ

Optional 15-Minute Extension

After scoring and a short discussion, the group may decide to add 15 more minutes of play. Continue from where you left off, then rescore and discuss what changed.
