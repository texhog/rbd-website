Work as a Team!

Resilience by Design is a collaborative workshop experience. From preparation to reflection, here's how teams work together to build climate resilience.

1

Co-Create Your Challenge

Together, select hazards relevant to your region, discuss local vulnerabilities, and co-create community resilience goals that reflect your shared values.

2

Play Together

Collaborate during a timed 1-hour session. Take resources, reserve projects, and complete them for points. Communicate openly and trade strategically to achieve your goals.

3

Measure Success

Calculate your team's total score from completed projects and achieved goals. Did you meet or exceed your community target? Celebrate wins or discuss what could be improved.

4

Reflect & Apply

Debrief as a group. What strategies worked? What didn't? Use the Community Resilience Planning Canvas to translate game insights into real-world climate action plans.

Choose Your Roles

Then, select 4 of 7 available roles to sit at the table. Each has unique abilities and trade powers to contribute to the team.

Local Government

Reserve up to 4 projects. Convert Materials to Volunteers.

Local Authority

Reserve up to 4 projects. Convert Materials to Volunteers.

Business & Industry

Pay less Funding on Level 2-3 projects. Draw random tokens.

Infrastructure Owners

Pay less Materials on Level 2-3 projects. Draw random tokens.

First Nations

Treat Knowledge as Political Will. Bonus points on combined costs. No Wild Aid cap.

Province

Take 4 different tokens. Program Transfer ability.

Community

Treat Volunteers as Materials. Gain Knowledge on Volunteer projects.
